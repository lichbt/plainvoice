## Why Freelancers Leave Invoice Simple

The pattern across G2 and Capterra reviews is consistent. Three friction points dominate the negative feedback — and none of them are minor.

### 1. The 2-Invoice Cap Hits Before You've Found Your Rhythm

Invoice Simple allows approximately two invoices on its free plan before the upgrade paywall appears. For a new freelancer still working out their process — testing numbering formats, figuring out their payment terms, deciding what goes in the notes field — two invoices disappear in minutes. Capterra reviewers repeatedly describe this as a bait-and-switch: the app is marketed as free, but the limitation isn't disclosed prominently before sign-up. When you're learning [what to include on an invoice](/blog/what-to-include-on-an-invoice/) for the first time, running into a paywall mid-draft is exactly the wrong experience.

### 2. Upgrade Prompts Interrupt the Invoicing Workflow

G2 reviewers leaving 2- and 3-star ratings specifically call out where the upsell prompts appear: inside the invoice editor, not tucked away on a pricing page. That's a deliberate placement decision. It interrupts you at the exact moment you're focused on getting a document out the door. One recurring complaint is being stopped mid-line-item to deal with an upgrade modal. For a task that should take two minutes, that friction is unacceptable.

### 3. Table-Stakes Features Are Paywalled

Recurring invoices, automated payment reminders, and online payment links — Invoice Simple treats all three as premium features. For any freelancer billing a retainer client or managing ongoing projects, these aren't extras. They're basics. Getting your [invoice payment terms](/blog/invoice-payment-terms-freelancers/) right and automating overdue follow-up is fundamental business hygiene. Charging extra for the privilege is the friction that pushes freelancers to look elsewhere.
