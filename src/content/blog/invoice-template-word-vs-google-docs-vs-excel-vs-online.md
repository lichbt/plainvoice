### Who Should Use Each Tool?

- **Word** — You already have a professionally designed template you've used for years and you invoice fewer than five clients a month. Switching tools would take longer than the problem it solves.
- **Excel** — You want a combined invoice-plus-bookkeeping tracker in one file, you're comfortable with formulas, and you don't need a slick client-facing PDF on a deadline.
- **Google Docs** — You live in Google Workspace, your clients are familiar with Drive links, and version history matters more to you than automation.
- **Plainvoice** — You're sending your first invoice, you bill multiple clients, or you want a professional result in under two minutes without touching a spreadsheet.

## Setup Time: The Hidden Tax on Your Billable Hours

Every minute spent adjusting column widths in Word is a minute you're not billing. Starting from a blank document means sourcing or building a template, resizing tables, adding your logo, and choosing fonts — easily 30 minutes before you've typed a single line item.

Excel adds formula risk on top of the setup burden. Delete one cell reference by accident and your subtotal breaks silently — you might not notice until the client questions the math. Google Docs is faster thanks to its template gallery, but you're still customizing branding before it looks professional.

Plainvoice eliminates the setup step entirely. Open the page, enter your name and your client's, add line items, and you have a clean professional invoice. ## Auto-Calculations: Where Word Falls Completely Flat

Word has no spreadsheet engine. Every total, tax line, and subtotal is a number you type by hand. Mistype one digit on a $2,000 invoice and you've either undercharged yourself or triggered an awkward correction email to a client you're trying to impress.

Excel solves this perfectly — it's what spreadsheets were invented for. But Google Docs shares Word's blind spot: it's a word processor, not a calculator. Real auto-math in Google's ecosystem means switching to Sheets, which has a completely different layout and its own learning curve.

Plainvoice calculates the moment you enter a quantity and unit rate. Add a tax percentage and every line and the grand total update instantly. No formulas to write, no cells to lock, no silent errors hiding until payment day.

## Client Delivery: Shareable Links vs PDF Attachments

How your client receives the invoice shapes how quickly they act on it. An attachment they have to download, open in desktop software, and file creates friction — and friction delays payment.

Word and Excel both require an export step: Save As PDF, locate the file, attach to email. That's three actions every single time. Google Docs closes the gap — a Share button generates a view-only link in seconds, though it lands inside a Google Drive interface that occasionally trips up less tech-savvy clients.

Plainvoice generates a permanent, clean invoice URL for every invoice you create. Your client clicks the link, sees a professional invoice page, and can download their own PDF copy if needed. ## Invoice Numbering and Tracking: The Feature Freelancers Skip Until They Regret It

Duplicate invoice numbers are an accounting problem — for you and your client's finance team. Word gives you zero help here. Google Docs gives you the same amount of nothing. You're keeping a sticky note on your monitor or hoping your memory is better than it is.

Excel lets you build a tracker yourself with a reference column and a MAX formula — but that's 20 minutes of setup that most freelancers skip, meaning they're back to the sticky-note system inside a spreadsheet.

Plainvoice auto-increments every invoice number from the moment you create your first one. The dashboard shows which invoices are outstanding, paid, or overdue across every client. Google Docs mobile is the best of the traditional tools here. The app is stable, formatting holds across device sizes, and sharing is two taps. It's a genuine differentiator for Google's ecosystem.

Google Docs handles this cleanly: full version history, automatic saves, accessible from any device with a browser. It's the strongest argument for staying inside Google Workspace if you're already there.

Plainvoice is cloud-native by design. Every invoice lives server-side, accessible via its unique link, and completely immune to local-file mishaps. There's no "did I save the latest version?" because there's only one version — the live one.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best free invoice template format for freelancers?

For most freelancers, an online invoice generator like Plainvoice is the best option — no download, no formatting, automatic calculations, and a shareable link sent directly to the client. Word and Google Docs templates work well if you already have one you like, but they require manual math and an extra PDF export step every time you bill.

### Can I use a Word invoice template professionally?

Yes — a well-designed Word invoice template looks completely professional to clients. The limitation is workflow, not appearance: Word has no auto-calculations, no invoice tracking, and requires a manual PDF export before you can email it. If you send invoices infrequently and already have a template you trust, Word is fine. If you invoice multiple clients regularly, the manual steps add up fast.

### Does Google Docs have a free invoice template?

Google Docs includes a basic invoice template in its template gallery, and it's a solid starting point. You get version history, auto-save, and easy link sharing — but no automatic calculations (Docs is a word processor, not a spreadsheet). For math, you'd need to switch to Google Sheets, which is a separate product with a different layout.

### How does an online invoice generator compare to an Excel invoice template?

Excel is the strongest offline tool for auto-calculations — its formula engine handles tax, subtotals, and discounts natively. But an online generator like Plainvoice matches Excel on math while adding features Excel can't: auto-incremented invoice numbers, a paid/unpaid dashboard, one-click shareable links, and full mobile responsiveness. Excel stays relevant for freelancers who want a combined invoice-and-bookkeeping file in one place.

### How do I keep track of invoice numbers when using a Word or Google Docs template?

You have to do it manually — maintain a separate log (a spreadsheet or even a notes app) and increment the number yourself each time. This works fine at low volume but becomes error-prone as your client list grows. Duplicate invoice numbers create accounting headaches for both you and your client. Online generators like Plainvoice auto-increment every invoice number and store the history in a dashboard, eliminating the manual tracking step entirely.
